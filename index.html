Step 6 of 6 — Your Briefing

Before You Begin

The outgoing executive director pulls you aside for one last conversation.

👨‍💼 Avi Rosen · Outgoing Executive Director
"Let me be honest with you about what you're walking into. This job is a balancing act — and the board is always watching."
📊 Your Stats Are Your Lifeline
You're managing three core metrics: Community Trust, Staff Morale, and Donor Confidence. Every decision you make affects them — sometimes in ways you don't expect.
🚨 How You Get Fired
If any stat drops to 20 or below, the board calls an emergency meeting and your tenure ends immediately. Watch your weakest stat — that's where crises will hit you hardest.
⏳ Entropy Is Real
Every round, your stats naturally decay by 2–4 points as community attention fades, donors expect fresh results, and staff energy flags. You have to keep running just to stay in place. Progress requires constant effort.
👥 You Can't Please Everyone
"Community Trust" isn't one number — it's the weighted average of how different groups feel about you: Orthodox, Reform, Conservative, unaffiliated, young adults, older adults. A decision that thrills one group may alienate another. Watch the segment breakdown carefully.
🏆 How You Win
At year's end, the board evaluates your overall performance. Score above 80 and you'll be offered a promotion to lead a national organization. Score below 40 and your contract won't be renewed. In between? You survived — but just barely.
⭐ Mission Matters
Your mission stars reflect how consistently you've stayed true to your stated priorities. Lose all your stars and the board loses confidence in your direction. Earn bonus stars through mission-aligned decisions and they'll boost your year-end score.
👨‍💼 Avi Rosen
"One more thing — there are no perfect choices in this job. Every decision has a cost. The best leaders are the ones who know which costs they can afford."
